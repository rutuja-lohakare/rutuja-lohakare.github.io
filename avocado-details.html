Avocado Price Prediction and Analysis

Introduction

Avocado has become one of the world's trendiest foods. According to the poster child of millennial healthy eating, this super food is now a mainstay for foodies everywhere. Avocado prices have rocketed in recent years by up to 129%, with the average national price of a single Hass avocado reaching $2.10 in 2019, almost doubling in just one year. The main objectives of this study were to predict avocado prices using the data gathered by Hass Avocado Board (HAB) - a trade organization that provides industry professionals with research and data on avocado prices around the world. Next is to find out pattern in attributes that affect the prices of avocados.

The project undertakes a comprehensive analysis of avocado prices, utilizing a robust dataset to uncover underlying patterns and predict future prices. The focus lies in understanding the factors affecting avocado prices and leveraging data analysis and machine learning techniques for predictive modeling.

Data Extraction and Cleaning

The initial phase involves extracting data from a large dataset, followed by rigorous data cleaning. This step is crucial to prepare the data for analysis by removing or modifying incorrect, incomplete, irrelevant, duplicated, or improperly formatted data. Such meticulous data cleaning ensures accuracy in the subsequent analysis and modeling stages.

Exploratory Analysis

The exploratory analysis phase concentrates on identifying seasonal patterns in avocado prices. This part of the analysis uses time series forecasting techniques to observe constant patterns that frequently occur year to year and month to month in both conventional and organic types of avocados. Tools such as dplyr, ggplot2, and DataExplorer in R are utilized for this analysis, enabling a detailed understanding of the data's temporal dynamics.

Applying DMBI/ML Algorithms

  • Linear Regression: Linear regression is employed to predict the value of a continuous variable (Y) based on one or more input predictor variables (X). The aim is to establish a mathematical relationship between the response variable and the predictors. The model is used to ascertain the importance of features by examining their coefficients. A significant coefficient indicates a spaner impact of the corresponding feature on the prediction.
  • Decision Tree Regressor: This algorithm is used to further classify and predict avocado prices. The dataset is split into training and testing sets, and the decision tree is built on the training set. Predictions are then made on the testing set, and the model's accuracy is evaluated using metrics like Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE), Mean Squared Error (MSE), and Mean Absolute Error (MAE). A lower MSE indicates that the model's predictions are accurate and close to the actual values.

Results

The results from linear regression confirm the hypothesis that avocado prices tend to decrease as volume sold increases, with the negative coefficient being statistically significant. Both conventional and organic types of avocados follow this trend. The decision tree regressor also shows good predictive accuracy, as indicated by the low MSE value. These results are pivotal for understanding market dynamics and can guide decision-making in the avocado supply chain.

Conclusion

The project demonstrates the effectiveness of using R for analyzing and designing various algorithms on datasets. R's capabilities extend beyond predictive modeling to include exploratory data analysis and data preprocessing. It's particularly adept at handling large datasets and can be used to work on smaller subsets of data to prevent issues like overfitting. The application of both linear regression and decision tree algorithms provides a comprehensive view of the factors influencing avocado prices and their prediction.
