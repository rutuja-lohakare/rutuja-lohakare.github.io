Customer Segmentation

Introduction

In today's competitive retail landscape, understanding customer behavior is pivotal for crafting targeted marketing strategies. This capstone project delves into customer segmentation, wielding data analysis techniques to unlock valuable insights about mall customer behavior. By leveraging a real-world dataset and K-Means clustering, this project focuses on understanding mall customer behavior in the competitive retail landscape. The primary objective is to utilize customer segmentation as a tool for crafting targeted marketing strategies.

Dataset Description

The dataset contains customer data that comprises five attributes, namely customer loyalty, which is the idea assigned to the customer visiting the mall, gender of the customer, age of the customer, and income and spending score of the customer visiting the mall.

Data Analysis and Preparation

The project starts with the collection and preparation of a real-world dataset. This involves cleaning the data, handling missing values, and ensuring the data is suitable for analysis.

Customer Segmentation using K-Means Clustering

The core of the project is the application of the K-Means clustering algorithm. This technique is used to segment customers into distinct groups based on their spending habits and preferences.

Insights and Implications

The analysis identifies various customer segments, each with unique characteristics. These insights are crucial for marketers to design personalized campaigns.

Conclusion

The project concludes by emphasizing the importance of data-driven insights in optimizing marketing strategies. The findings empower marketers to tailor their efforts more effectively, maximizing the impact of their campaigns in the retail sector.
