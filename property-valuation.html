Residential Property Valuation

Introduction

This project tackled the challenge of ensuring equitable and transparent property valuation in Cook County, Illinois. It aimed to develop a statistical model that could accurately predict fair market prices, particularly in lower socioeconomic areas. The project endeavors to provide fairer property assessments for homeowners, ultimately contributing to a more just and transparent valuation process.

Data Preparation

  • Data Cleaning: Large datasets like "historic_property_data" containing 50,000 recent sales and a "codebook" defining relevant variables were utilized. The process involved removing non-predictive attributes according to the codebook and eliminating duplicate entries to ensure data integrity.
  • Missing Value Imputation: To address missing data, the Last Observation Carried Forward (LOCF) method was used for time-sensitive features like neighborhood and town codes, while the mean of respective columns was used to impute missing numeric values.
  • Variable Selection: Focusing on location dimensions and flood risk, the selection process prioritized using numerical scales to avoid conversions and facilitate analysis. Correlation matrices and Variance Inflation Factors (VIF) helped identify and remove highly correlated variables, preventing the model from being misled by redundant information.

Modeling and Evaluation

A combination of modeling techniques was employed, including linear regression, random forest, and lasso regression. Cross-validation methods were meticulously applied to enhance the models' accuracy and ensure their robustness across different data subsets. This exploration of diverse techniques allowed for a deeper understanding of the data's underlying patterns and relationships.

The final stage involved a rigorous evaluation of the models' performance using Mean Squared Error (MSE) as the primary metric. This metric quantified the difference between predicted and actual values, providing insights into the models' effectiveness in accurately estimating fair market prices. The evaluation also identified areas for further improvement, such as incorporating additional predictors related to construction quality to refine the model's accuracy further.

Conclusion

This project adopted a comprehensive and data-driven approach, combining advanced data preprocessing techniques, strategic variable selection, diverse modeling methods, and thorough evaluation. This multi-faceted approach aimed to address the challenges faced by the Cook County Assessor's Office in predicting fair market prices for homes, ultimately contributing to a more equitable and transparent valuation system in the county.
