Supply Chain Logistics Data Analysis

Introduction

This project involves a comprehensive analysis of a Supply Chain Logistics dataset, focusing on analyzing a Supply Chain Logistics dataset to enhance decision-making and optimize processes within the sector. By creating a data dictionary, addressing vital business questions, and developing predictive models using machine learning techniques, the project aimed to derive actionable insights for improved efficiency and profitability.

Methodology

  • Data Cleaning and Preparation: Data quality was ensured through cleaning and preparation, creating a solid foundation for subsequent stages.
  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): In-depth analysis was conducted to understand the data's characteristics and identify trends.
  • Machine Learning Modeling: Predictive models were developed to address specific business questions and provide actionable insights.
  • Model Evaluation: Performance evaluation using metrics like accuracy and precision ensured the reliability of generated insights.

Business Questions

Five key business questions were addressed.

  1. Plant Capacity vs. Daily Order Demand
    • Analysis: Daily order data was compared with plant capacities to determine if plants operate at full capacity and can handle demand efficiently.
    • Findings: Plants 04, 13, and 16 met their daily order demand, while others experienced overcapacity. This highlights the need for capacity optimization or order redistribution to enhance efficiency and avoid delivery delays.
  2. Plant Capacity vs. Daily Order Demand

    • Findings: Plants 04, 13, and 16 met their daily order demand, while others experienced overcapacity. This highlights the need for capacity optimization or order redistribution to enhance efficiency and avoid delivery delays.
  3. Carrier and Route Delays
    • Analysis: Carriers and routes with the highest shipment delays were identified. Additionally, average delays across service levels (Door to Door, Door to Port, Customer Referred Freight) were examined.
    • Findings: Carrier 'V444_0' and the 'DTP' service level displayed the highest estimated delays. This information is crucial for addressing logistical challenges and improving delivery efficiency, particularly for DTP services.
  4. Shipping Volume Distribution
    • Analysis: The weight distribution per route and service level handling high volumes was investigated, along with the top 10 products sold per route.
    • Findings: The DTP service level managed the highest volume on the Port 04 to Port 09 route, while CRF handled the most significant volume on the Port 09 to Port 09 route. This information facilitates informed decisions regarding route and service level planning based on volume demands.
  5. Cost-Effectiveness of Carrier Transportation Options
    • Analysis: The most cost-effective carrier options and specific modes (air, ground) were examined.
    • Findings: Carrier V444_0 was more cost-effective for air travel, whereas Carrier V444_1 was found to be cost-effective for aerial transport. This guides informed decision-making about carrier selection for different modes, maximizing cost efficiency.
  6. Customer Order Volume
    • Analysis: Customers placing the highest number of orders and contributing significantly to the total order share were identified.
    • Findings: 9 out of the top 46 customers accounted for 80% of daily demand, highlighting the importance of maintaining strong relationships with these key customers and potentially tailoring services to their specific needs.

Conclusion

This project utilized data analytics and machine learning to provide valuable insights into various aspects of supply chain logistics. Each business question was meticulously analyzed, offering information that contributes to informed decision-making, efficient resource utilization, improved customer satisfaction, and overall profitability in the supply chain management sector.
